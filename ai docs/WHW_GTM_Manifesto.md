## 1. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Who We Serve

Independent Buy Here, Pay Here (BHPH) dealerships in the United States — owner-operated, typically running 25–75 cars per month, with an in-house portfolio of 100–400 active accounts. The owner is on-site daily and personally tracks delinquency, often alongside a spouse or a small collections staff of 1–3 people.

### Firmographic Profile

- **Business type:** Independent used-car dealership with in-house financing (BHPH or LHPH)
- **Deal volume:** 25–75 cars/month (sweet spot: 30–54)
- **Portfolio size:** 100–400 active accounts, ~$1.6M–$6.4M in notes receivable
- **Team size:** 2–6 people total — owner, spouse, 1–2 collectors, lot staff
- **DMS systems:** iDMS, DealerCenter, Wayne Reaves, DealerPack, AutoManager
- **Geography:** All U.S. states; concentrated in TX, FL, GA, OH, IL, NC, MO

### Psychographic Profile

This dealer knows their charge-off problem is real and getting worse — SGC Accounting confirmed 2024 was the worst year on record for the industry. They feel the pain every month-end when they run the numbers. They are not opposed to technology, but they are skeptical of anything that sounds like a "magic fix" and have limited time to implement or manage new systems.

Jim Rhoads has publicly identified the leading causes of charge-offs in the BHPH Success Facebook group: Non-payment/no contact, Mechanical problems, Lost job or income, Accidents, and Bankruptcy. Three of these five — non-payment/no contact, mechanical issues, and employment changes — are addressable before they become a loss, if the dealer knows about them early enough.

## 2. Jobs to Be Done, Pain Points & Value Proposition

### Current Workflow: What the Dealer Does Today

A BHPH dealer's collection process is almost entirely reactive. A payment misses. A collector calls. If no response, the GPS is checked. If the car is located, a field rep is dispatched for a repo. By the time action is taken, the account is 30–60+ days past due, the customer has often already lost their job, wrecked the car, or mentally moved on. The dealer absorbs the loss.

The dealer knows early intervention works. Joe Powell describes it on the BHPH Morning Show: "we'd much rather work with them sooner and get them back on track." But the team is small, the portfolio is large relative to capacity, and the signals — a customer mentioning the engine light came on, a job change, a hardship — surface in random phone calls or not at all.

### The Pain Points

- **No systematic early warning:** Dealers only find out a customer has lost their job or has a car problem when the payment stops.
- **Collector bandwidth:** 1–2 collectors managing 100–400 accounts cannot proactively touch every customer regularly.
- **Charge-offs are expensive:** At $16K average receivable, even saving 2 accounts/month is $32K preserved per month.
- **Mechanical issues kill accounts:** Car breaks down → customer frustrated → stops paying → repo → charge-off. Kaleb Bryan (1,600 accounts) explicitly asked how to "reduce charge offs due to vehicle issues."
- **Employment is the silent killer:** Jim Rhoads lists "Lost job/income" as a primary charge-off category. Dealers have no way to detect this early.
- **Manual collection is inconsistent:** Collections quality depends entirely on the individual collector's skill and time. Gene Daughtry: "it's still the manager understanding everything you want done."

### Value Proposition

> **White Hat Way Early Warning** helps BHPH dealers catch the early warning signs of a failing account — mechanical stress, employment changes, financial hardship — before the payment stops, so the human team can intervene while the relationship is still savable.

## 3. Pricing

### The ROI Math

A dealer running 54 cars/month at a 39% charge-off rate against monthly sales loses approximately 21 accounts/month. At $16,000 average receivable per account, that's $336,000/month in gross receivables at risk. Even after repo recovery (typical net recovery: $4,000–$8,000 per unit), the net loss per charged-off account runs $8,000–$12,000.

| Scenario | Accounts Saved/mo | Value Preserved |
|---|---|---|
| Conservative (10% reduction) | ~2 accounts | ~$20,000/month |
| Moderate (15% reduction) | ~3 accounts | ~$30,000/month |
| Strong (20% reduction) | ~4 accounts | ~$40,000/month |

### Pricing Recommendation

Position this as a subscription with tiered pricing based on portfolio size. The ROI story needs to be simple and concrete: "Save 2 accounts a month — the tool pays for itself 20x over."

| Tier | Portfolio Size | Monthly Price | Break-even |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | Up to 150 accounts | $299/mo | Save 1 account every 2 months |
| Growth | 150–300 accounts | $499/mo | Save 1 account/month |
| Portfolio | 300–500 accounts | $799/mo | Save 1–2 accounts/month |

These prices are intentionally conservative relative to value delivered. A dealer running 54 cars/month with ~200+ active accounts landing in the Growth tier at $499/month needs to save less than 1 account every 30 days to break even. The pricing should be easy to say yes to.

## 4. The Solution

### Product Concept: BHPH Early Warning AI Agent

An AI-powered agent that maintains ongoing, lightweight contact with each active BHPH customer — via text message or outbound call — with one purpose: to surface early warning signals before the account fails. It then delivers a structured, actionable alert to the human collections team so they can intervene while the relationship is still alive.

### How It Works

1. **Connects to the dealer's DMS** to import active accounts, payment schedules, and vehicle details.
2. **Sends regular AI-driven check-ins** via SMS (or voice) to each customer — friendly, branded, short. Cadence is configurable (e.g., weekly or bi-weekly). These are not collection calls. They are relationship touchpoints.
3. **Listens for warning signals** in customer responses — car trouble, job loss, financial hardship, relocation, dissatisfaction. The AI is trained to recognize and categorize these signals.
4. **Surfaces a daily or real-time alert** to the dealer or collector — *"Customer [Name] mentioned the transmission is slipping. Recommend proactive service outreach before next payment due date."*
5. **Human team takes action** with full context. The AI hands off — it does not resolve the situation. This keeps the White Hat Way philosophy of human relationships at the center.

### Key Product Features

- **Two-way SMS or voice AI agent** with natural language understanding
- **Signal detection engine** — trained on BHPH-specific risk events: car trouble, employment change, hardship, ghosting risk
- **Daily alert digest** for collections staff — prioritized by urgency
- **DMS integration** with iDMS, DealerCenter, Wayne Reaves, and others via API or file import
- **Payment reminder layer** — proactive reminders 2–3 days before due date, reducing missed-payment friction
- **Compliance guardrails** — all messaging is TCPA-compliant; opt-in captured at deal signing
- **Dashboard and reporting** — monthly view of accounts flagged, interventions made, and accounts saved

### What the Agent Does NOT Do

It does not replace the collector. It does not make collection demands. It does not negotiate. It does not disable vehicles. It is an early-warning layer that makes the human team smarter and faster — consistent with the White Hat Way philosophy of proactive, relationship-first collections.

## 5. Go-to-Market Motion

### Distribution Strategy

BHPH dealers do not discover new tools through cold email or banner ads. They discover them through the people they already trust — other dealers, industry podcasters, conference presenters, and 20 group peers. The White Hat Way channel is already positioned inside that trust network, which is the primary unfair advantage.

### Channel 1 — White Hat Way Audience (Primary)

- **The BHPH Morning Show** — introduce the tool in episodes, feature early-user dealer stories, host live demos on-air
- **White Hat Way community and email list** — direct announcement to an existing warm audience of BHPH operators
- **BHPH Nation platform** — list the Early Warning Agent as a featured resource/tool on the platform

### Channel 2 — Facebook Group (BHPH Success)

The BHPH Success group (10,000+ members) is where the target ICP actively posts about charge-offs, collections, and operational pain. Posts from Jim Rhoads already receive strong engagement. A structured content presence — short posts about the cost of late intervention, dealer-specific ROI math, poll questions about charge-off causes — can warm the market before any sales pitch.

### Channel 3 — Conference and Event Presence

- **NIADA annual convention** — booth and/or speaking slot; dealers come specifically to find solutions to portfolio problems
- **BHPH United, TIADA, and state association events** — Jim and Michelle are already speakers in this circuit
- **BHPH Dealer Forum (Indianapolis format)** — roundtable format is ideal for showing the tool in context of real dealer problems

### Channel 4 — Podcast Co-marketing

- **The Independent Dealer Podcast (Luke Godwin & Jeff Watson)** — already aligned with WHW, large listener base, open to partnership
- **Sponsored segment or guest appearance** — frame as "how we think about the charge-off problem and what we built to solve it"

### Sales Motion

| Stage | Activity |
|---|---|
| Awareness | Content on Facebook, Morning Show episodes framing the charge-off problem and early-intervention opportunity |
| Interest | Live demo on the Morning Show or at a conference; dealer watches the product in action with a real account scenario |
| Trial | 30-day free pilot for 5–10 early adopters; dealer adds 10–20 accounts; sees first alerts within 2 weeks |
| Conversion | Simple monthly subscription via credit card; no long-term contract required to start |
| Expansion | Dealer adds full portfolio; refers peers in their 20 group or state association |

### Purchasing Pattern

- **Payment method:** Monthly credit card subscription — this audience pays for GPS, CPI, DMS, and CRM tools this way already
- **Contract:** Month-to-month to start; annual pre-pay option at a 10–15% discount
- **Decision maker:** The owner — no procurement, no IT, no committee. One conversation, one decision
- **Typical objection:** "Show me the math and let me try it" — the 30-day pilot directly addresses this

### Early Adopter Positioning

Frame the first 20 dealers as founding members. They get a reduced rate locked in permanently ($199/month instead of $299+), white-glove onboarding, and their name in the story when the product is formally launched. The BHPH space is relationship-driven — being part of something early matters to these operators.
